Research

What I Work On

Two interconnected threads — intelligent network security and trustworthy multimodal AI — with a shared question: can machines be both capable and accountable?

Primary Thread

Wireless Intrusion Detection Systems

My doctoral dissertation built increasingly capable WIDS by integrating LASSO-hybrid feature selection, human-expert rule fusion, automated ensemble methods with synthetic sample generation, and adversarial robustness evaluation against EJSMA+DDPG attacks. The core contribution: a framework that reduces false alarms through knowledge integration while staying deployable via decentralised training.

↗ GitHub: AWID Feature Reduction
Current Work

Trustworthy Multimodal Deception Detection

A framework fusing audio, video, text, and EEG through a human behaviour concept bottleneck and causal-inspired cross-modal attention. Achieves ROC-AUC 0.94±0.008 on RLDD+EEG benchmark, outperforming MulT by 7 points. Human-model agreement κ=0.61 vs. SHAP baseline κ=0.34.

Under review: The Visual Computer, Springer (Major Revision)

↗ GitHub: Deception Detection
Applied Research

Self-Supervised Autism Diagnosis

Self-supervised learning strategy using unlabelled video of typical behaviours + small labelled autism corpus. Three proxy tasks guide the model, including a repeated-behaviour detection proxy for recognising recurring stereotypical patterns.

↗ GitHub: Autism Diagnosis
Applied Research

Cultural Heritage Image Inpainting

GAN + diffusion architecture (WSFN + SCN + DIMD discriminator) for restoration of temple murals from Tamil Nadu. Addresses colour bias while preserving artistic integrity — evaluated on custom datasets of endangered cultural artefacts.

Applied Research

Sign Language Recognition

Morpho-syntactic analysis pipeline translating Tamil sentences to American Sign Language gloss via neural machine translation and motion graphs, broadening accessibility for regional-language users.

Applied Research

Multi-LLM Automated Essay Scoring

Multi-LLM pipeline for rubric generation and self-reflective grading that adapts to question-response variability, addressing the limitations of fixed-rubric approaches in AI-based education assessment.

Future Research Directions

01

Explainable Intrusion Detection (XIDS)

Evolving WIDS into a fully explainable IDS that articulates classification rationale per flagged traffic instance. The natural convergence of both primary research threads.

02

Formal Causal Multimodal AI

Advancing from causal-inspired design to formally identified causal effects via structural causal models and backdoor-criterion-admissible DAGs. Target: NeurIPS, ICML, IEEE TPAMI.

03

Neurological Condition Screening

Extending the physiological concept-bottleneck pipeline to ADHD, autism, and PTSD screening via the RIKEN Brain Science Institute collaboration. Target: IEEE JBHI, Nature Machine Intelligence.
